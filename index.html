Gameplay — Smooth or Same Old?

The gameplay is smoother than FC 24/25. Passing feels cleaner, dribbling isn’t stiff, and players don’t move like refrigerators anymore.

  • Across-the-box shots? Still OP.
  • Cut-ins with pacey wingers? Yup, still here.

Verdict: Improved, not reinvented.

Career Mode — Upgrade or Copy-Paste?

EA promised “major changes.” Here’s what actually changed:

  • Tiny UI updates
  • More cutscenes
  • Slightly better youth system
  • Managers still look like NPCs made at 2 AM

It’s not bad… it’s just okay.

Ultimate Team — Pay or Cry

  • Packs still overpriced
  • Promos every 5 minutes
  • 82-rated goalkeepers when you wanted Mbappé
  • Small cool features added, but core issues remain

If you don’t spend money, prepare for pain. If you do spend money, prepare for more pain.

Graphics — Better, Not Mind-Blowing

The graphics got a small polish:

  • Stadiums look nicer
  • Player faces more accurate
  • Lighting is cleaner

But it’s not a next-gen jump.

AI — Sometimes Smart, Sometimes Drunk

Defenders now react faster and block more shots. Teammates still occasionally forget what football is.

  • CBs stepping forward randomly
  • Fullbacks wandering into midfield
  • Keepers diving the wrong way

Final Verdict — Should You Buy It?

Buy it if:

  • You skipped FC 25
  • You play with friends
  • You’re addicted to UT

Don’t buy it if:

  • You already have FC 25
  • You only play Career Mode
  • You are broke

⭐ Score: 7/10 — Fun? Yes. Worth $70? Debatable.
